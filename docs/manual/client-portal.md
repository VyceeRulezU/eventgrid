# Client Portal

**Route:** Generated per-client link (no fixed route)

---

## The Big Picture

Your client doesn't use EventGrid. But they still want to see:
- What's happening with their event
- Who the vendors are
- What the schedule looks like

The Client Portal gives them a **read-only view** without needing an account.

---

## Creating a Portal Link

1. On the **Event Details** page, click **Client Portal**
2. Enter:
   - **Client's name**
   - **Client's email**
   - **Client's phone** (optional)
3. Click **Generate Portal**

A unique link is created. **Copy it** and send it to your client.

### Link Format
```
https://eventgrid.ng/portal/wedding-abuja-ab1c
```

The link uses a **slug format**: client name + short code. Easy to share and remember.

---

## What the Client Sees

When your client opens the link, they see:

| Section | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Event name & date** | Overview of the event |
| **Vendors** | List of vendors booked (name + category) |
| **Run sheet** | Event schedule / timeline |
| **Progress** | Phase completion status |

Everything is **read-only**. The client cannot make changes.

---

## Best Practices

- Send the portal link **2-3 weeks before the event**
- Re-send it **a few days before** so they can check the run sheet
- If client details change, generate a **new portal link**
- The link works on **any device** (phone, tablet, computer)

---

## What Coordinators See

Coordinators **cannot** generate or manage client portals — only planners can.

---

## Pro Tips
- Personalise the message when you share the link
- Use the portal to **build excitement** — share vendor reveals
- The portal updates **automatically** as you update the event
