# Guest Management

**Route:** `/events/:id/guests`

---

## The Big Picture

You're throwing a party and 100 people are coming. You need to:
- Know **who** is coming (RSVPs)
- **Where** they sit (seating chart)
- **Check them in** at the door

The Guest Management page does all this.

---

## Guest List

### Adding Guests
| Method | How |
|--------|-----|
| **Manual** | Click **Add Guest**, fill in name, phone, email |
| **CSV Import** | Click **Import CSV**, drop a file with columns: name, phone, email, table |

### Guest Fields
- **Name** (required)
- **Phone** (optional)
- **Email** (optional) — used to match clients
- **Table** (optional) — assign to a table
- **VIP** — flag important guests
- **Plus One** — let them bring someone

### RSVP Status
| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Haven't replied yet |
| **Confirmed** | Said yes |
| **Declined** | Said no |
| **Maybe** | Not sure yet |

Click the status badge to cycle through options.

### Check-in
When guests arrive, click **Check-in** to mark them as arrived. Checked-in guests are highlighted.

---

## Seating

### Tables
1. Go to the **Seating** tab
2. Click **Add Table** — give it a name (e.g. "Table 1", "VIP Table") and capacity
3. **Assign guests** to tables from the guest list

### Visual Layout
The seating view shows tables as cards. The guest list on the side lets you drag or assign guests to seats.

---

## Counts & Stats

At the top of the page:
- **Total Guests** — all guests
- **Confirmed** — RSVP'd yes
- **Declined** — RSVP'd no
- **Pending** — haven't replied
- **Checked In** — arrived at event

---

## What Coordinators See

Coordinators can **view** the guest list and check guests in, but cannot add, edit, or delete guests or tables.

---

## Pro Tips
- **Import CSV** before the event to save time
- Use **VIP flag** for special treatment guests
- **Check guests in** as they arrive to track attendance in real time
